Work Experience

This page briefly goes over the exposition of two short experiences.

Military

In 2014, and 2015, I was a sworn-in solider of Her Majesty. My identity, at that time, was as follows: 5th Division, 45th Engineer Squadron, 36th Combat Engineers, Service Number A54 947 668, Pte. Moore. My job, as an engineer, was to mainly dismantle IEDs, be a construction and explosion, and combat expert. Through BMQ (Basic Military Qualifications), SQ (Soldier’s Qualifications), and DP1 (Trades Course), I learned many skills, tranferable to civilian life: teamwork, leadership, communication, interpersonal relationship building, and so on. Likewise, I was a pretty good shot on the range. In some ways, I miss serving in the Canadian Armed Forces, but in other ways, I cannot serve anymore. While on course, I sustained life-changing injuries to my feet. My left foot sustained a fallen tranverse arch, and my right foot sustained the same, though to a lesser degree. The skills I learned in the military served me well, and I thank my staff who pushed us all forward, to be the best soldiers possible, and also the Queen, for her support. These skills, likewise, prepared me to endure didactic, and practical, education that each Nova Scotia paramedic must perfect for graduation.
